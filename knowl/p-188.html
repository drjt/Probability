Since successive trials are independent, then the probability of the rth success occurring on the mth trial presumes that in the previous m-1 trials were r-1 successes and m-r failures. Therefore the desired probability is given by \begin{gather*} P(X=m) = \binom{m - 1}{r-1}(1-p)^{m-r}p^r \end{gather*}
