From the computation before, the mean of this data is about 6.33 million residents. So, to determine the variance you may find it easier to compute using the theorem above. \begin{align*} v & = \left ( \frac{\sum_{k=1}^n y_k^2 }{n} \right ) - \mu^2 \\ & \approx \frac{4434.37}{50} - (6.33)^2 \\ & = 48.72 \end{align*} and so you get a sample variance of \begin{equation*} s^2 \approx \frac{50}{49} \cdot 48.72 = 49.71\end{equation*} and a sample standard deviation of \begin{equation*}s \approx \sqrt{49.71} = 7.05\end{equation*} million residents.
