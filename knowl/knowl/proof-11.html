Proof

Using \(z = \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\) gives \(x = \sigma z + \mu\) resulting in

\begin{align*} E[X] & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} x \cdot e^{- \left ( \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right )^2 / 2} dx\\ & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}(\sigma z + \mu) e^{-z^2 / 2} dz \\ & = \sigma \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} z e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + \mu \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z^2 / 2} dz \\ & = \sigma \cdot -e^{-z^2/2} \Big |_{-\infty}^{\infty} + \mu \cdot 1\\ & = 0 + \mu \end{align*}

Therefore, the provided parameter \(\mu\) is indeed the mean of the distribution.
