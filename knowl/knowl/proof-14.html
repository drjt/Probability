Proof
\begin{align*} f’(x) & = \frac{-1}{\sigma^3 \sqrt{2\pi}}e^{- \left ( \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right )^2 / 2} \cdot (x-\mu)\\ \Rightarrow f’’(x) & = \frac{-1}{\sigma^3 \sqrt{2\pi}}e^{- \left ( \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right )^2 / 2} \cdot \left [(x-\mu) \cdot (\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}) \cdot (\frac{1}{\sigma}) - 1\right ] \\ & = \frac{-1}{\sigma^3 \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{- \left ( \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right )^2 / 2} \cdot \left [ (\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2 - 1 \right ] \end{align*}

Setting this second derivative equal to zero to determine the locations of possible points of inflection yields \(\displaystyle (\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2 = 1\) or \(\displaystyle \Big | \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma} \Big | = 1\) or finally \(\displaystyle x = \mu + \sigma\) and \(\displaystyle x = \mu - \sigma\).
