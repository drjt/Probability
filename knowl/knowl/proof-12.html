Proof

Once again by using \(x = \sigma z + \mu\) yields (using integration by parts on the final integral)

\begin{align*} Var(X) & = E[X^2] - \mu^2 \\ & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} x^2 \cdot e^{- \left ( \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right )^2 / 2} dx - \mu^2\\ & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}(\sigma z + \mu)^2 e^{-z^2 / 2} dz - \mu^2\\ & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \sigma^2 z^2 e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} 2\sigma z \cdot \mu e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \mu^2 e^{-z^2 / 2} dz - \mu^2\\ & = \sigma^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} z^2 e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + 2\sigma \mu \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} z e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + \mu^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z^2 / 2} dz - \mu^2\\ & = \sigma^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} z \cdot z e^{-z^2 / 2} dz + 2\sigma \mu \cdot 0 + \mu^2 -\mu^2\\ & = \sigma^2 \left [ -z e^{-z^2/2} \Big |_{-\infty}^{\infty} - \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} -e^{-z^2 / 2} dz \right ] + 0\\ & = \sigma^2 \left [ (0-0) - (-1) \right ]\\ & = \sigma^2 \end{align*}
